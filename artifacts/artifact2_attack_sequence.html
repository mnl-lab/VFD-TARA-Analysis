▶ SCENARIO 2 / CYBER-PHYSICAL ATTACK
S2M Magnetic Bearing
Rotor Crash
Attack sequence · VFD TARA case study · BSI TR-03183-1 alignment
ATTACKER OBJECTIVE
Cause immediate, unrecoverable physical destruction to a 30 MW centrifugal gas compressor via cyber access.
SCENARIO ASSUMPTIONS
Remote vendor access is available, an engineering workstation can assume operational priority, and segmentation/monitoring controls are incomplete. The scenario models an adversary with OT process knowledge.
CRA EVIDENCE VALUE
Each phase ties attacker behavior to explicit violated controls, detection opportunities, and ATT&CK for ICS techniques. This supports compliance evidence and technical review.

03
SAFETY DISABLEMENT Disable AVR, ABS & UPS Via the engineering workstation, disables the MIMO/SISO control algorithms — specifically killing Adaptive Vibration Rejection (AVR) and Automatic Balancing System (ABS). Simultaneously accesses control cabinet power supply management to disable the UPS battery backup system. ▸ Eliminates both the vibration safety and the controlled-rundown capability
CONTROL CABINET / UPS MODULE UPS disabled; AVR/ABS off Power supply module confirms UPS deactivation. AMB control module no longer executing vibration compensation. System is now in an inherently unstable state — rotor levitation maintained only by static electromagnetic field. ▸ BSI FAIL: REQ_ER 9.3 (safety functions during network loss) REQ_ER 9.2 (resume after interruption) DETECT: watchdog on AVR/ABS/UPS state changes with dual-approval enforcement
PHYSICAL RISK ↑ Controlled rundown impossible Without UPS, a power-loss event cannot execute the safe electromagnetic rundown sequence. 10-minute battery buffer gone. Rotor at full speed with no vibration control.
▼ KINETIC FAILURE
04
TRIGGER Drop Electromagnetic Levitation Commands high-power amplifiers (300V / 30A per axis) to immediately zero the electromagnetic levitation field. The 30 MW rotor drops under gravity at full operational speed. ▸ Amplifiers rated 300V · 30A per axis — enormous kinetic energy stored in rotor
BACK-UP BEARINGS (BUB) Rotor contacts BUBs Multi-ton rotor contacts dry-lubricated sintered bush back-up bearings. BUBs rated for only a small number of high-speed rundowns. No vibration control → rotor impacts at maximum eccentricity. UPS cannot supply controlled rundown current. ▸ BUBs engineered for short-duration low-duty drops — NOT uncontrolled high-eccentricity impacts DETECT: hard interlock trigger on levitation-current collapse and unsafe rundown profile
CATASTROPHIC Rotor crash · BUB disintegration Kinetic energy exceeds thermal + mechanical tolerance of BUBs within seconds. Back-up bearings disintegrate. High-speed rotor strikes static housing. 30 MW compressor destroyed. Extreme kinetic + potential explosive hazard to facility. ▸ Estimated cost: tens of millions USD + production halt + safety risk to personnel
